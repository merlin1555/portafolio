What is PLA?

PLA, or polylactic acid, is a type of biodegradable plastic that comes from renewable sources such as corn starch or sugar cane. Unlike traditional plastics derived from petroleum, PLA decomposes under suitable conditions in composting, converting into water, carbon dioxide and organic matter. This means that it is not only environmentally friendly by reducing dependence on fossil fuels, but also contributes to reducing the accumulation of waste in our landfills and oceans.

The Importance of PLA in Chile

Chile is taking giant steps towards sustainability and environmental responsibility. The adoption of PLA takes on special relevance in this context, offering an ecological and sustainable alternative to conventional plastics. Its use in a variety of applications, from food packaging to disposable products, represents a significant change in how we think about and use materials in our daily lives, aligning us with a greener and more sustainable vision.

Getting ahead of the Change

With the Extended Producer Responsibility Law (REP) and growing awareness about the environmental impact of plastics, Chile is quickly moving towards a more sustainable future. Adopting PLA is not only an ecological decision; It is also a smart strategy to get ahead of change. Companies and consumers who choose PLA are not only complying with current legislation, but are also preparing for a market that will inevitably demand more sustainable practices and environmentally friendly products.
